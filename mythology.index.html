1. Divine Birth

Lord Krishna was born in Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva in a prison cell, as prophesied to destroy the tyrant Kansa, Devaki's brother. On the night of His birth, Vasudeva secretly carried Krishna across the Yamuna river to Gokul, where He was raised by Yashoda and Nanda Maharaj.

His eternal love with Radha is considered the highest symbol of devotion. The Ras Leela between Krishna and the Gopis represents divine love and joy.

Krishna returned to Mathura, defeated and killed Kansa, and freed His real parents. He then established law and dharma in the kingdom.

To protect His people, Krishna built the city of Dwarka under the sea. He married Rukmini and later other queens, establishing a divine kingdom of peace and righteousness.

After the Yadava clan destroyed itself due to a curse, Krishna left His mortal body in Prabhas Kshetra. His departure marked the end of the Dwapara Yuga and the beginning of the Kali Yuga.

10. Eternal Lord

Lord Krishna is worshipped as the Supreme Personality of Godhead. His life is not just a tale, but a guide to living a righteous life with love, wisdom, and devotion.
